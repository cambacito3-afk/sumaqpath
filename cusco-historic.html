Surrounding to Cusco

Cusco

Base camp at altitude

Cusco is the natural hub for any Peruvian adventure. The San Blas neighbourhood offers the best balance of character, location, and value — colonial architecture, artisan workshops, and a short walk to the Plaza de Armas. Book at least a week ahead during peak season.

Hostel

San Blas hostels

  • Terraced views over tiled roofs; short walk to Plaza de Armas.
  • Ask for rooms away from the street for quieter nights.
  • Price range: ~$10–20 USD per night (dorm)
Mid-range

Colonial guesthouses

  • Converted Inca-Spanish buildings with courtyards and wood beams.
  • Breakfast usually included — fuel up before long hiking days.
  • Price range: ~$40–80 USD per night
Budget tip

Arrive early, negotiate

  • Walk-in rates in low season (Nov–Apr) are often 30–40% cheaper.
  • Avoid booking platforms that charge high commissions to small hostels.

The Andes

Sleep among the mountains

Outside Cusco, accommodation becomes simpler and more authentic. Homestays with local families around Lake Titicaca and in Colca villages offer a window into Andean daily life that no hotel can replicate. Expect basic but clean facilities, and bring cash — cards are rarely accepted.

Homestay

Lake Titicaca islands

  • Sleep in a reed house on Uros or Amantaní with a local family.
  • Dinners are simple, hearty, and perfect after long days on the water.
  • Price range: ~$15–30 USD including meals
Village stay

Colca Canyon villages

  • Chivay and Yanque have small guesthouses near hot springs.
  • Wake up early for condor sightings at Cruz del Cóndor viewpoint.
  • Price range: ~$20–50 USD per night
Trek camp

Salkantay campsites

  • Dome tents and mountain lodges along the Salkantay route.
  • Sky Lodge options offer glass sleeping pods over the Sacred Valley.
  • Price range: ~$30–150 USD depending on comfort level

The Amazon

Deep in the jungle

Peru's Amazon basin — centred on Puerto Maldonado and Iquitos — offers some of the most biodiverse wilderness on earth. Eco-lodges here bundle accommodation with guided wildlife walks, river canoe trips, and night safaris. The further from town, the better the wildlife sightings.

Eco-lodge

Puerto Maldonado

  • Lodges bundle river transport and guided walks in Tambopata Reserve.
  • Pack biodegradable repellent and a headlamp — humidity is relentless.
  • Price range: ~$80–200 USD per night (all-inclusive)
Remote

Iquitos jungle lodges

  • Only reachable by boat or small plane — no roads connect Iquitos to the rest of Peru.
  • Multi-day packages include pink river dolphin and macaw clay lick visits.
  • Price range: ~$100–300 USD per night
Budget

Town guesthouses

  • Stay in Puerto Maldonado town and book day tours to the reserve.
  • Less immersive but significantly cheaper than deep-jungle lodges.
  • Price range: ~$15–40 USD per night

Booking Tips

Book smart

A few principles go a long way when booking accommodation in Peru. Avoid the big platforms where possible — small family guesthouses get squeezed by commission fees, and booking direct almost always gets you a better deal and a warmer welcome.

Timing

High vs low season

  • Peak season is June–August — book 4–6 weeks ahead in Cusco and Aguas Calientes.
  • Rainy season (Nov–Apr) means fewer crowds and prices drop 20–40%.
Payment

Cash is king

  • Many small guesthouses and homestays only accept Peruvian soles.
  • Withdraw cash in Cusco or Lima before heading to remote areas.
Safety

Store valuables safely

  • Ask for a locker or safe at check-in — most hostels provide them free.
  • Keep a photocopy of your passport separate from the original.
