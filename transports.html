Getting around Peru

Move light: combine buses for budget legs, trains for scenic windows, and helicopters for the extraordinary.

By Bus

The budget backbone

Buses are the backbone of backpacker travel in Peru. Long-distance routes connect all major cities, with overnight options that save on accommodation costs. Always choose a reputable company with reclining seats on the lower deck for smoother rides on mountain roads.

Overnight

Cusco → Puno

  • Night bus with 160° seats; choose lower deck for smoother ride.
  • Arrive for floating islands on Lake Titicaca at dawn.
  • Journey time: approx. 6 hours · ~$15–25 USD
Scenic

Cusco → Arequipa

  • Crosses the high altiplano at over 4,300 m — bring a layer.
  • Cruz del Sur and Oltursa are the most reliable operators.
  • Journey time: approx. 9 hours · ~$20–35 USD
Local

Valle Sagrado hops

  • Collectivos between Pisac, Urubamba, and Chinchero run all day.
  • Carry coins; fares are fastest with exact change.
  • Journey time: 30–60 min · ~$1–3 USD

By Train

Scenic rails through the Andes

Peru's train routes are among the most spectacular in South America. The journey from Ollantaytambo to Aguas Calientes winds through cloud forest and river gorges — a destination in itself. Book well in advance, especially during high season (June–August).

Classic

Ollantaytambo → Aguas Calientes

  • Vistadome cars give the best river-and-jungle frames.
  • Book the earliest slot to beat mid-morning crowds at Machu Picchu.
  • Journey time: approx. 1h 45min · ~$50–80 USD
Luxury

Hiram Bingham Train

  • Orient-Express style service with dining car and live music.
  • Includes brunch on the way up and cocktails on the return.
  • Journey time: approx. 3h 30min · ~$500 USD
High altitude

Cusco → Puno by Rail

  • Andean Explorer crosses Lake Titicaca basin at 3,800 m.
  • Full-day scenic journey with stops at Raqchi and La Raya pass.
  • Journey time: approx. 10h · ~$150–300 USD

By Helicopter

Peru from above

For those who want to see Peru's most iconic landscapes from a completely different perspective, helicopter transfers and tours offer an unmatched experience. Fly over the Nazca Lines, access remote Inca sites, or arrive at Machu Picchu in style — skipping roads entirely.

Iconic

Nazca Lines flyover

  • The only way to truly appreciate the giant geoglyphs in the desert.
  • Flights depart from Nazca or Ica airport daily, weather permitting.
  • Duration: 30–45 min · ~$80–150 USD
Premium

Cusco → Machu Picchu

  • Fly over the Sacred Valley and land at Aguas Calientes helipad.
  • Cuts travel time from 3+ hours to under 30 minutes.
  • Duration: ~25 min · ~$300–500 USD per person
Remote

Amazon jungle access

  • Reach eco-lodges deep in the Madre de Dios that have no road access.
  • Operated by lodge providers from Puerto Maldonado airport.
  • Duration: 20–60 min · price varies by lodge

General Tips

Travel smart

Whatever transport you choose, a few rules apply across the board in Peru: always book in advance during June–August high season, carry small bills for local transport, and allow extra time for altitude-related delays on mountain roads.

Money

Always carry soles

  • Local buses and collectivos only accept Peruvian soles in cash.
  • ATMs are scarce outside major cities — withdraw before you travel.
Altitude

Acclimatize first

  • Spend at least 2 days in Cusco (3,399 m) before heading higher.
  • Altitude sickness can affect anyone regardless of fitness level.
Booking

Plan ahead in high season

  • Train and helicopter seats sell out weeks in advance June–August.
  • Use official operator websites — avoid street touts for long-distance routes.
